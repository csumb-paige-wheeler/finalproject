Leah

First of all, welcome to CSUMB! I hope you’re enjoying your time here so far. If you’ve come to this page for advice, I hope you find mine helpful!

Academics

The main thing to watch out for academics-wise is keeping up with your assignments, projects, and exams for your classes! It’s easy to feel a lack of motivation to keep up with schoolwork or even attendance, especially since many classes have ongoing assignments, but it is important not to let it build up too much so that you don’t get overwhelmed during midterms and finals. A planner really helps, as does using Google Calendar effectively to block out times to work on assignments.

Extracurriculars

Extracurriculars at CSUMB include clubs, athletics, and other organizations. I encourage joining an affinity group to find community, especially if you have moved from another area. I, personally, joined APIA, which has provided me with a sense of community and belonging since I am not from this area.

I personally do not have much experience working with Recreation, who are in charge of athletics and other outdoor activities, but I encourage joining their events to gain community and get outdoors!

Working & School

From my time in Community College and working, I have definitely had my share of experience balancing both. While having a job may be necessary, it is also important to set aside time for yourself in order to not burn yourself out. Time blocking comes in handy especially while balancing school and a job, in order to dedicate time to self-care. If possible, I would also encourage trying to find an on-campus job such as a student assistant. On-campus jobs are typically more understanding of your class schedule and the time needed to study.

Clubs

Finding a stable group of friends or people to hang out with is a gamble at this school because; 1. You already have those friends since day one, or 2. You feel like you never will have those friends. The bestest way of getting around this hoop is investing free time into clubs and the members involved. This is the definitive way of finding other students with similar likings. You really just have to talk (I know, it's hard), but once you learn how, it's smooth sailing from there.!

Google Calendar

Please, please, PLEASE do not wait until it's too late to use Google Calendar. If you are like me, living on campus, you will find yourself not knowing what to do because, often times, there isn't much to do. Start putting your life on a schedule, make things easy for yourself, and build good, healthy habits!

Express Yourself

More generally, but most importantly; Be yourself and express yourself for who you are. Once you show your community who you are, you'll find people that love you for it. All in all, be genuine to others and yourself. Be you.

With that being said, there are a few things that I would love to give you advice with while you're first starting on your journey.

Connections

Making connections is crucial to success in college. Building relationships with classmates, professors, and others in your field allows you to expand your network, gain insights into your field of study, and discover new perspectives. It may even lead to mentorship opportunities, internships, and job offers after graduation. Attend networking events, join clubs, and strike up conversations with your peers to take advantage of these opportunities.

Tutoring

Struggling with college coursework? Feeling overwhelmed and stressed? A tutor can make all the difference! Tutors provide personalized support, help you understand difficult concepts, and offer study strategies to boost your confidence. CSUMB offers free tutoring through CLC, but if that's not for you, online tutors are also available. Seeking a tutor is a smart move that shows you're taking control of your education. Reach out to your professor or campus tutoring center today!

Talk to your Professor

In college, your professors can be your allies! Despite what you might think, they're not intimidating or scary - they're there to help you learn and succeed. If you're struggling with a particular topic or assignment, don't hesitate to ask for help. You can schedule office hours or send them an email - just be polite and explain your situation clearly. Your professor will appreciate your initiative and dedication to learning. So take a deep breath and give it a try!
